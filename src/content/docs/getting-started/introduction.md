Welcome to the **I2 Research Guide**, a resource created by [Interactive Intelligence](https://i2-research.org) (I2) to help researchers, students, and practitioners navigate the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and neuroscience.

## What is I2?

Interactive Intelligence (I2) is a research organization dedicated to advancing the understanding of intelligent systems — both biological and artificial. Our work spans foundational theory, applied methods, and interdisciplinary research at the intersection of computer science and neuroscience.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide is organized into three main research domains:

- **AI Research** — Covers modern AI architectures and techniques, including transformers, large language models, and prompt engineering. These topics represent the cutting edge of what AI systems can do today.

- **Machine Learning** — Explores the core methods that underpin AI systems: supervised learning, neural network design, and optimization. Understanding these fundamentals is essential for anyone working in the field.

- **Neuroscience** — Examines how biological neural systems process information, from computational models of the brain to brain-computer interfaces. These insights inspire and inform the design of artificial systems.

## Who This Guide Is For

Whether you are a beginning researcher looking for an introduction to these fields, a student preparing for coursework, or an experienced practitioner seeking a reference, this guide provides accessible yet rigorous coverage of each topic.

Each article includes key concepts, explanations of core ideas, and pointers to further reading for deeper exploration.
